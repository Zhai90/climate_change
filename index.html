What?

What is climate change?

Climate change is caused by greenhouse gas coming from smoke and carbon dioxide (CO2) mostly produced from factories, machines such as cars and other transportation devices that use fuel as a way to power them. We've been using these machines too much for too long, so long that it has been building a wall around our planet Earth, causing the weather and climate to change, for example, the wildfires are happening each year more frequently due to either thunderstorms or human mistakes, and our glaciers are melting extremely fast due to the temperature rising. Learn More!

Why?

Why do we need to take action?

"Why do we have to help?" is a very good question! Do you remember about our weather and climate changing because of a wall made out of CO2 forming around our planet? The Sun's heat is getting inside that wall, and isn't coming out, meaning that every year, the weather will be hotter and hotter, until one day, it will be way too hot, the air will be extremely polluted and we will run out of water because of it evaporating and will not be able to survive anymore on Earth. We need to take action now before it's too late or the people in the future won't be able to see Earth!


How?

How can we help?

That's the attitude! Some very small things you can change will add up and make a big difference! For example, if you live near your school, instead of using the car, use a bike or walk to school, both are very healthy, or if you want, you can take the bus, which can take a lot of people in 1 single vehicle, which means that less CO2 will be produced.
Ask your parents about "solar panels"! They are little square panels that you put on the roof of your house to gather the energy from the Sun and transforms it into electricity you can use! They are a bit expensive, but it's an investment!

Who?

Who are the responsible for this?

Everyone. Everyone is, this is our home planet after all, we decide its future, we are its future. As we told you before, you can do it, with very little effort, it piles up and makes a great difference.
